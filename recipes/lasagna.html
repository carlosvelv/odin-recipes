Description

This recipe guides you through building a classic, comforting lasagna from the ground up. It features layers of a rich, slow-simmered meat sauce (ragù) made with ground meat, tomatoes, and herbs. This savory sauce is layered between tender lasagna noodles and a creamy béchamel (or ricotta) white sauce, all generously topped with melted mozzarella and sharp Parmesan cheese. The dish is baked until it's golden-brown, bubbly, and perfectly combined, resulting in a hearty and flavorful Italian-American staple.

Ingredients

  • Ground Beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Carrots (finely chopped)
  • Celery (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Canned Crushed Tomatoes
  • Tomato Paste
  • Red Wine (optional, for deglazing)
  • Olive Oil
  • Dried Oregano
  • Dried Basil (or fresh)
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Butter
  • All-Purpose Flour
  • Milk (whole)
  • Freshly Grated Nutmeg
  • Salt
  • Whole Milk Ricotta Cheese
  • Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • One Large Egg
  • Fresh Parsley (chopped)
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Lasagna Noodles (oven-ready or regular)
  • Shredded Mozzarella Cheese
  • Grated Parmesan Cheese

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce: Sauté the onion, carrots, and celery in olive oil until soft. Add the garlic.
  2. Add the ground meat and brown it, breaking it apart. Drain any excess fat.
  3. (Optional) Deglaze the pan with red wine.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and herbs (oregano, basil).
  5. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat, cover, and let it cook for at least 1 hour (longer is better) for flavors to meld. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Make the béchamel: Melt butter in a saucepan, whisk in flour, and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  7. Slowly whisk in the milk until smooth. Bring to a simmer, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens.
  8. Remove from heat and stir in salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  9. (Alternative) Make the ricotta filling: In a bowl, mix the ricotta, Parmesan, egg, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  10. If using regular (not oven-ready) noodles, cook them in salted, boiling water until al dente. Drain and lay them flat.
  11. Preheat your oven (e.g., 375°F or 190°C).
  12. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a large baking dish.
  13. Arrange a single layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  14. Spread a layer of the béchamel (or ricotta mixture) over the noodles.
  15. Add another layer of meat sauce.
  16. Sprinkle with a layer of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  17. Repeat the layers (Noodles > Béchamel/Ricotta > Meat Sauce > Cheeses) until the dish is full, finishing with a final layer of sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan.
  18. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil.
  19. Bake for 25-30 minutes.
  20. Remove the foil and bake for another 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden-brown and the sauce is bubbling.
  21. Let the lasagna rest for 15-20 minutes before slicing and serving. This helps it set.
